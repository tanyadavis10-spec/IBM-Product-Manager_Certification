## Lab Overview

This lab covered the Market Requirements Document, the structured output of the market and solution investigation that captures validated customer needs, the competitive landscape, and what the product must do to address the market problem. It translates research into a prioritized set of requirements that guide the product into the Plan phase.

The MRD is organized around four key areas: the targeted market segment, the market problem, the requirements the product must meet, and a prioritization of those requirements by market importance. The course emphasized that the MRD focuses on what the market needs, not how the product delivers it. That distinction keeps the team grounded in customer problems before moving to engineering solutions.

## Market Requirements Document - Prep&Pivot App

---

### Section 1 - Market Segment

**Targeted Market Segment:**
Meal preppers, nutrition enthusiasts, and busy users who want simple meal planning and macro tracking.

**Specific Buyers and Users:**
People who want easy food logging, personalized nutrition guidance, and flexible meal substitutions when schedules change.

---

### Section 2 - Market Problem

- Many users struggle with manual tracking, inaccurate food entries, and time-consuming nutrition planning.
- Existing nutrition tools often lock important macro features behind tiered pricing and provide limited flexibility when routines change.
- There is limited availability of AI agentic nutrition coaching solutions that can proactively build plans and help users adjust meals intelligently while staying aligned with macro goals.
- Users need an easier way to stay on target with macros while receiving adaptive coaching, planning support, and quick adjustments throughout the day.

---

### Section 3 - Market Requirements

- Market requirements include an AI agentic nutrition coach that can create daily nutrition plans, guide food choices, and help users stay aligned with macro goals.
- Planning and tracking capabilities should include meal planning, macro tracking, barcode/search food logging, custom goals, saved meals, favorite foods, and a daily dashboard.
- The dashboard should also surface AI-generated insights based on user trends, informed by relevant medical journals and scientific research, to provide more personalized nutrition guidance.
- The AI coach should use user history to recommend macro-matched meal swaps and adapt plans when schedules or meal options change.
- Users expect simple onboarding, reliable food data, strong technical performance, and transparent all-in-one subscription pricing.
- Success measures include plan approvals, diary additions from approved plans, meal-swap usage, retention, and customer satisfaction.

---

### Section 4 - Prioritization

Requirements prioritized by perceived market importance:

1. AI agentic nutrition coaching that creates adaptive daily plans
2. AI-generated dashboard insights based on user trends and scientific research
3. Flexible nutrition planning and macro tracking capabilities
4. Macro-matched meal swaps when plans change
5. Transparent pricing and reliable app performance

---

## Key Skills Demonstrated

- Market requirements documentation — translating research and discovery into a prioritized, structured requirements document
- Market segmentation - identifying the targeted buyer and user profiles with specificity
- Problem articulation - documenting the market problem in customer terms before defining any solution
- Requirements prioritization - ranking requirements by market importance rather than technical convenience
- Success measurement - defining how the product will know it is meeting market needs

## Key Takeaway

In my operations career, I work with VOC data regularly, reviewing inbound contact trends and customer feedback to identify friction points and make recommendations. That experience made the market problem section of this lab feel natural because I am used to listening to what customers are telling you through their behavior and translating that into something actionable. What was new was learning how to formalize that same thinking into a structured requirements document for a product I am developing. The prioritization section was the most challenging part. I had to make a deliberate decision about what matters most to the target market for Prep&Pivot, not just list everything I want to build. Ranking the AI agentic nutrition coaching capability at the top, above even the tracking features, reinforced that the AI concierge is not just a feature of this app, it is the reason the app exists. That clarity was useful and is something I will keep coming back to as development moves forward.
