# Lab: Resolve Issues Through Effective Communication
**Course:** IBM Product Manager Professional Certificate — Course 2  
**Module:** 1 - Effective Collaboration & Stakeholder Engagement  
**Completed:** March 2026

---

## Lab Overview

This lab presents a real-world product management scenario and challenges you to identify communication and collaboration breakdowns - then recommend specific tools and frameworks to resolve each one. The scenario follows Priya, a new Product Manager navigating the Plan phase of the product lifecycle while managing a team with unclear roles, unprepared stakeholders, competing priorities, and document management challenges.

---

## Scenario Summary

Priya has just stepped into the Product Manager role for a new beauty product concept. Her team is in the Plan phase and facing multiple communication challenges simultaneously  -role confusion causing duplicated work, stakeholders showing up to meetings unprepared, no clear process for prioritizing product versions, goals that lack measurable criteria, a product vision that is too long, and documents that are impossible to find. Each issue represents a distinct breakdown in collaboration that a product manager must diagnose and solve using the right tool or framework.

---

## My Analysis — Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Root Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Duplicated efforts during the Conceive phase | Role and responsibility confusion - no clear ownership across the team | Create a RACI matrix that clearly outlines and defines each stakeholder's responsibilities |
| Unprepared attendees and limited meaningful input in meetings | Stakeholders not given adequate time or structure to prepare before sessions | Use Brain Writing to give stakeholders time to prepare ideas independently before group discussion |
| Product versions not prioritized before launch | Prioritization was overlooked during conception - now a reactive problem | Apply the Nominal Group Technique to systematically prioritize product versions as a team |
| Ongoing disagreements about goals and how success will be measured | Goals lack specificity and shared definition - stakeholder collaboration breaking down | Use a goal checklist to ensure all goals satisfy SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) |
| Two-page product overview too long - CEO needs a concise version | Product vision not distilled into a clear, compelling narrative | Apply the Six Sigma elevator speech method to develop a concise Product Vision that engages partners and buyers |
| Documents difficult to locate and distinguish | No consistent document management or naming system | Adopt a naming convention methodology to organize and identify documents consistently |

---

## Key Skills Demonstrated

- **Root cause analysis** - diagnosing the underlying cause of each communication breakdown rather than just describing the symptom
- **Framework selection** - matching the right tool to each specific problem (RACI, Brain Writing, Nominal Group Technique, SMART goals, Elevator Speech, naming conventions)
- **Stakeholder management** - understanding how communication failures affect different stakeholder groups differently
- **Product lifecycle awareness** - recognizing which phase each problem belongs to and why earlier resolution prevents downstream issues
- **Practical problem solving** - applying course frameworks to a realistic product management scenario

---

## Key Takeaway

What stood out most in this lab is that every issue Priya faced had a specific, proven framework designed to prevent it - and most of them were problems I recognized from my own operational experience. Role confusion, unprepared meeting attendees, unclear success metrics, and document chaos are not unique to product management. They show up in any complex, cross-functional environment. What this lab gave me is the product manager's toolkit for addressing them proactively rather than managing the fallout after they've already slowed the team down. The shift from reactive problem-solving to proactive framework design is one of the most transferable skills I'm building through this certification.
