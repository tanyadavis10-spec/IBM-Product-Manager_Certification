## Lab Overview

An elevator pitch is a powerful tool for delivering a precise and concise overview of a product - outlining its purpose, target audience, and what distinguishes it from competitors. The objective is to immediately capture the audience's attention and convey the most significant aspects of your product vision.

This lab challenges you to select a product and develop a compelling elevator pitch using the standard Product Vision format — defining the audience, the problem, the product, the value proposition, the competitive differentiation, and the ultimate benefit.

---

## My Product — Prep&Pivot

For this lab I developed an elevator pitch for **Prep&Pivot** - an AI-powered nutrition tracking and meal planning app designed for people who need flexibility, not friction, when managing their nutrition goals.

---

## Product Vision Elevator Pitch

| Element | Prep&Pivot |
|---|---|
| **For** | Meal preppers, nutrition enthusiasts, and people on the go who need to adapt when life doesn't go according to plan |
| **Who** | Need a flexible, intelligent way to plan and track their macros without being locked behind paywalls or complicated pricing tiers |
| **Our Product** | Prep&Pivot - an AI-powered nutrition tracking and meal planning app |
| **That** | Allows users to plan meals, track macros, and pivot on the fly with the help of an AI nutrition concierge - all included at one price |
| **Unlike** | MyFitnessPal's complicated tiered plans that charge extra for features users actually need |
| **Prep&Pivot** | Delivers a full-featured AI nutrition experience - planning, tracking, and real-time pivoting - at one transparent price with no hidden upgrades |

---

## Key Differentiators

**AI Nutrition Concierge** - not just a food logger but an intelligent assistant that helps users adapt their nutrition plan in real time when schedules, cravings, or circumstances change

**All Features, One Price** - eliminates the frustration of tiered pricing that puts core functionality behind a premium paywall

**Built for Real Life** — designed specifically for users who plan ahead but need flexibility when life happens - the "pivot" is the product

---

## Competitive Positioning

| Feature | Prep&Pivot | MyFitnessPal |
|---|---|---|
| Macro tracking | Included | Basic free / Premium paid |
| AI nutrition guidance | Included | Limited / Not available |
| Meal planning | Included | Premium tier |
| Pricing model | One flat price | Tiered - free + paid upgrades |
| Target user | Flexible planners who need to adapt | Broad general fitness audience |

---

## Key Skills Demonstrated

- **Product vision development** - translating a product idea into a structured, compelling pitch
- **Competitive differentiation** - clearly articulating what makes a product different and better than the alternative
- **Audience definition** - identifying a specific target user with a specific pain point rather than a generic demographic
- **Value proposition framing** - connecting product features to the customer outcome that actually matters
- **Elevator pitch format** - applying the industry-standard For / Who / Our Product / That / Unlike / Value structure

---

## Key Takeaway
Prep&Pivot is a product I'm currently developing, which made this lab particularly valuable. Writing the elevator pitch early in the development process forced me to get specific about something that's easy to leave vague — what value the product will truly deliver to the customer and how it's meaningfully different from the competition. Before completing this lab I had a clear idea of what Prep&Pivot would do. After completing it I had a clear articulation of why a specific customer should choose it over MyFitnessPal. That distinction between describing features and communicating value allowed me to develop a stronger vision for the prodcut, and this framework gave me a repeatable way to do it.
