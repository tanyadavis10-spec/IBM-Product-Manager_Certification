## Lab Overview

This lab introduced the Project Charter as the document that formally authorizes a product project and establishes the product manager's authority to apply resources toward the work. It defines team composition, purpose, objectives, KPIs, sponsor, deadlines, resources, and meeting cadence before execution begins.

The module also covered the Tuckman Ladder, Forming, Storming, Norming, and Performing, as a model for how teams move through predictable development stages. Designing for those stages intentionally, rather than waiting for friction to resolve on its own, is what separates a well-structured team launch from one that loses momentum early.

What stood out most in this lab is how much the charter does before a single line of work is started. Every section creates a shared agreement on who is responsible, what success looks like, when things are due, and how the team will communicate. Without that foundation, teams spend time later re-establishing things that should have been decided at the start.

## Initial Project Charter — Prep&Pivot App

---

### Team Composition

- Product manager / project lead
- UX/UI designer
- Mobile app developer(s)
- AI/ML integration specialist
- QA tester
- Marketing and customer support representatives

---

### Team Purpose

Design, build, and launch Prep&Pivot for My LLC as a mobile app that simplifies meal planning, macro tracking, and meal adjustments through an all-in-one subscription and an agentic AI nutrition concierge.

**Information statement:** Prep&Pivot will help meal preppers, nutrition enthusiasts, and busy users stay consistent with nutrition goals by combining easy logging, flexible meal planning, and smart macro-matched pivots when routines change.

---

### Objectives

- Deliver an MVP with meal planning, macro tracking, barcode/search food logging, custom goals, saved meals, favorite foods, and a daily progress dashboard.
- Integrate an AI nutrition concierge that can build daily plans from user history and suggest meal swaps that stay within macro targets.
- Launch a beta release, gather user feedback, and refine usability, personalization, and technical performance before full release.
- Differentiate My LLC with transparent all-in-one pricing and reduced manual tracking time compared with leading competitors.

---

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- App downloads and subscriber growth
- Daily and monthly active users, retention rate, and churn rate
- Average revenue per user and customer acquisition efficiency
- Agent-generated plan approvals, diary additions from approved plans, meal plan completion, and meal-swap usage
- Customer satisfaction ratings, app ratings, and technical performance indicators

---

### Sponsor

My LLC executive sponsor / founder — responsible for funding approval, strategic alignment, and launch readiness decisions.

---

### Deadlines

| Phase | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Conceive and Plan | August 15, 2025 – September 30, 2025 |
| Design and Development | October 1, 2025 – June 30, 2026 |
| Qualify (Beta) | July 1, 2026 – July 31, 2026 |
| Launch | August 1, 2026 |
| Post-Launch Optimization | August 2, 2026 – October 31, 2026 |
| Retire | To be determined based on product lifecycle and business performance |

---

### Resources

- Product budget for app design, development, testing, marketing, and customer support
- AI integration resources and a reliable food database
- Cloud hosting, analytics, and mobile development tools
- QA testing devices and app store deployment support
- User feedback channels for beta and post-launch improvement

---

### Meetings

- **Weekly team status meeting** — review progress, blockers, and priorities
- **Biweekly design and development review** — feature alignment and technical risks
- **Monthly sponsor update** — KPIs, budget, milestones, and launch readiness
- **Beta feedback review sessions** — before full launch and during post-launch optimization

---

## Key Skills Demonstrated

- Project charter development — formally documenting team structure, purpose, and authority before work begins
- Objective setting — defining specific, actionable outcomes that guide the team through the product lifecycle
- KPI definition — establishing the measures that will determine whether the product is succeeding
- Timeline and milestone planning — mapping each phase from Conceive through Retire with clear dates
- Meeting cadence design — building in structured communication that keeps stakeholders and the team aligned throughout

---

## Key Takeaway

I have spent years coordinating programs, launches, and team structures in operations, so some of what the Project Charter covers felt familiar. What was new was seeing it applied to a product I am building from scratch and understanding how the charter functions as a formal authorization document, not just a planning tool. The deadlines section was the most valuable part of this lab for me personally. Working backward from the August 1, 2026 launch date to define each phase, including a dedicated Qualify period for beta testing and a Post-Launch Optimization window, made the timeline for Prep&Pivot concrete in a way it had not been before. The Retire row also stood out. I had not thought about building an exit plan for a product before it even exists, but the course framed it as responsible product stewardship and that reframe made sense to me. Having a lifecycle plan for the whole product, not just the launch, is something I will carry into the development work ahead.
