## Lab Overview

This lab introduced the SWOT analysis as a formal product management tool used during the Conceive phase to evaluate whether a product concept is worth pursuing. The exercise required both an internal and external product assessment documented in a structured template. I applied it to the Prep&Pivot App, the AI agentic nutrition concierge with meal planning and macro tracking capabilities.

The SWOT framework has four dimensions: Strengths and Weaknesses are discovered through an internal assessment of factors the product controls directly, while Opportunities and Threats are discovered through an external assessment of the market environment outside the product's control. The course emphasized that both assessments are required and that the goal is an honest diagnostic, not a justification of a conclusion already reached.

---

## About the Product

**Prep&Pivot** is an app I am currently building. It is an AI agentic nutrition concierge with integrated meal planning and macro tracking capabilities. The AI concierge builds personalized daily plans using saved meals, diary history, and favorited foods, and helps users pivot with macro-matched meal swaps when schedules or plans change unexpectedly.

## SWOT Analysis

*Perform an internal and external product assessment and document findings using the SWOT template.*
*Product: Prep&Pivot App*

---

### Internal Assessment

**STRENGTHS**

- Clinical-level nutrition data accuracy
- Deep micronutrient tracking
- Robust privacy and security
- All-in-one pricing model - premium features and AI concierge included at a single price with no confusing tiers
- Agentic AI that builds daily plans from user history, saved meals, and favorited foods, then adds the approved plan directly to the diary
- Macro-matched meal swap suggestions including restaurant alternatives when plans change unexpectedly

**WEAKNESSES**

- Entering a saturated market with established brands and strong user loyalty
- New products slower to appear in the food database
- No community or social features
- Limited brand awareness as a new market entrant
- Monetization model not yet proven at scale

---

### External Assessment

**OPPORTUNITIES**

- Wearable integration expansion
- Targeting the chronic condition management segment
- Developing AI-driven insights and deeper personalization
- Growing consumer demand for health apps and meal planning tools
- Favorable market window as AI-assisted nutrition tools gain attention before competitors further strengthen user loyalty

**THREATS**

- Competitors strengthening their food and micronutrient databases
- Market saturation with strong incumbents - MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, MacroFactor
- AI-powered apps introducing photo logging and automated tracking features
- Brand loyalty to existing apps creating high switching costs for new entrants

---

## Key Skills Demonstrated

- SWOT framework application - conducting a structured internal and external product assessment
- Internal vs. external analysis - separating what the product controls from what the market presents
- Diagnostic thinking - using findings to surface real competitive position rather than justify a predetermined conclusion
- Strategic connection - identifying how strengths and weaknesses link to corresponding opportunities and threats

## Key Takeaway

I was introduced to SWOT in my marketing coursework and have used it throughout my career, including in my current operations role when presenting clients with information about rolling out new program features. What this lab taught me is how to apply it specifically as a product management tool in the context of a product I am actively developing. In my previous uses of SWOT, I was evaluating something that already existed. Here I was using it to assess whether Prep&Pivot had a strong enough foundation to move forward before anything is built. That shift in timing changes what the framework is asking you to do. The weaknesses and threats sections carry more weight at this stage because there is still an opportunity to address them in the product design, not just manage them after the fact. That was a genuinely new way of thinking about a tool I thought I already understood well.
