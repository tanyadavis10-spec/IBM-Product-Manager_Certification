## My Biggest Insight From This Course
My current skills align more closely with product management than I initially recognized. Cross-functional collaboration, stakeholder communication, data analysis, and building operational systems from scratch are capabilities I've developed at scale across 11 years and five programs. What this course helped me see is - I've been doing product operations work without the language or framework for it — translating customer friction data into platform recommendations, contributing requirements during tool evaluations, and participating directly in product and technology feedback sessions. The gap I'm closing isn't foundational — it's methodological. Agile, roadmap ownership, and user story writing are the product-specific frameworks I'm building through this certification.

The most important skill I'm developing is product sense — the discipline of starting with the customer question before jumping to the operational solution. My career has trained me to optimize systems that already exist. What I'm building is the complementary instinct of asking whether something should exist at all, and if so, what it should genuinely do for the customer. That shift from operational thinking to product thinking is the core of this transition.

My strengths coming in are building structure in ambiguous environments, translating data into action, and delivering consistent results under pressure. My honest challenge is that the same instinct that makes me effective operationally — solving problems quickly — can work against me in product contexts where slowing down to fully define the problem leads to better outcomes. I'm actively developing the discipline of problem definition before solution design.

This course also sharpened my thinking about what makes product management genuinely hard — influencing without authority, making defensible prioritization decisions under competing stakeholder pressure, and staying close to the customer as a product scales. That third challenge is where product operations becomes critical, and where my background positions me to contribute most directly. Every program I've managed was essentially a startup — built from a blank slate with a client expectation and little existing infrastructure. That repeated experience of building before being asked, measuring results, and iterating quickly is the entrepreneurial foundation I'm bringing into product management. The discipline I'm adding is knowing when to slow down, align stakeholders, and define the problem before building the solution.
