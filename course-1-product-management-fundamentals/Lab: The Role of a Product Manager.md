## Scenario Summary

IT Industrial Services is a 300-person company that has spent five years managing university learning platforms through a partnership with Learning, Inc. The CEO wants to expand beyond platform management into customized solutions for universities and community colleges — but the company has no product management function. As VP of Strategy, the task is to define the ideal product manager profile and build a product management framework from scratch.

---

## My Work

### Ideal Product Manager Profile for IT Industrial Services

**Skills Required:**

- **Leadership** — ability to align executives, engineers, customer support, and sales around a shared product vision without direct authority; confidently makes prioritization decisions while balancing customer needs and business objectives
- **Research** — gathers qualitative and quantitative data to identify gaps in current service offerings and opportunities for differentiation in the higher education market
- **Analytical** — analyzes market trends, usage data, customer feedback, and performance metrics to ensure product investments deliver measurable value
- **Communication** — translates customer needs into requirements, presents roadmaps to leadership, and tailors messaging for both technical and non-technical audiences
- **Teamwork** — collaborates across engineering, operations, customer success, and partner organizations including Learning, Inc.
- **Judgment** — balances short-term customer requests against long-term product strategy without compromising scalability or profitability

**Experience Required:**
- Market trend research in higher education technology and learning platforms
- Customer feedback collection through surveys, A/B testing, focus groups, and direct interviews
- B2B or service-oriented product background in IT or education-focused organizations

**Understanding of Development Process:**
- Full product lifecycle from discovery and definition through delivery and continuous improvement
- Familiarity with agile and iterative development processes
- Ability to prioritize backlogs, define requirements, and validate solutions before release

**Goals:**
- Expand IT Industrial Services beyond basic platform management into value-added customized solutions
- Increase customer retention by addressing institution-specific needs
- Drive sustainable growth through scalable, well-defined product offerings
- Strengthen competitive position in the higher education IT services market

**Vision for the PM Organization:**  
The product management organization acts as the strategic bridge between customers, technology, and business leadership — owning the product vision and roadmap, ensuring alignment with company strategy, and continuously refining offerings based on customer feedback and market changes. The goal is to move the organization from reactive service delivery to proactive product innovation.

**Criteria for Success:**
- Improved customer satisfaction and retention rates
- Clear, actionable product roadmaps aligned with company strategy
- Successful launch of new or enhanced service offerings
- Measurable business impact such as revenue growth or expansion into new market segments
- Strong cross-functional alignment and reduced friction between teams

---

### Benefits of Having a Product Manager

**How does it favor them against competition?**  
Having a product manager allows IT Industrial Services to proactively identify customer needs and market trends rather than reacting after competitors move first — resulting in more differentiated, customer-focused offerings that scale-focused competitors may not provide.

**How does it improve growth opportunity?**  
Product managers help transform services into repeatable, scalable products. This enables the company to expand its portfolio, increase revenue streams, and pursue new customer segments while maintaining consistency and quality.

**How does it position them in the market?**  
With strong product management, IT Industrial Services positions itself as a strategic partner rather than just a platform management provider — elevating the brand as an innovative, customer-centric organization capable of delivering tailored solutions for universities and community colleges.

## Key Takeaway

This lab reinforced that product management is fundamentally about creating alignment between what customers need, what the business can deliver, and where the market is heading. The most important insight for me was recognizing that a company can have strong operations and still fail to grow if no one owns the product vision and the roadmap that makes growth actionable. This is the core capability I am building toward in my own transition from operations leadership into product operations.
