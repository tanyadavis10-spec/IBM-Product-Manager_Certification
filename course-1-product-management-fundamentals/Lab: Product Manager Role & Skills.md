## Scenario Summary

Heavy Auto Manufacturer, Inc. wants to develop an AI-powered fuel management system for semi-trucks — a system that would regulate fuel consumption based on real-time conditions and potentially provide automated steering guidance. The CEO has connected with AI researchers at a state college to explore integration of their technology. As product manager, the task is to define the scope of work, identify risks, rewards, and challenges, and select the core skills needed to lead this product from concept to market.

---

## My Analysis

### Risks

- AI inaccurate calculations or instructions creating safety hazards for drivers
- Driver distraction from system alerts or interventions
- A competitor already implementing the same technology — limiting differentiation window
- Regulatory and compliance barriers for AI-assisted vehicle control

### Rewards

- Measurable reduction in fuel consumption and operating costs per trip
- Clear competitive differentiation from non-AI truck manufacturers
- Market leadership opportunity in AI-integrated commercial vehicles
- Potential to expand to other automated systems (braking, routing, load management)

### Challenges

- Implementation timeline — AI integration with physical vehicle systems is complex and slow
- Cost analysis — development, testing, and regulatory approval costs may be significant
- Driver and operator adoption — users may resist giving partial control to an AI system
- Validation and testing requirements before commercial deployment

### Stakeholders & Collaborators

- Engineering teams — hardware integration and system architecture
- AI researchers and data scientists — algorithm development and testing
- Safety, compliance, and regulatory experts — ensuring legal and safety standards are met
- Senior leadership — strategic alignment and budget approval
- Drivers and field testers — usability validation and real-world feedback

### Scope of Work

- Defining product requirements and constraints in collaboration with engineering
- Gathering driver and tester feedback to validate usability and safety
- Coordinating with developers to ensure accurate AI behavior and system integration
- Partnering with senior product manager to align execution with long-term strategy

## Skills Identified for This Role

From the course skills list, I identified the following as most critical for this product:

| Skill | Why It Applies |
|---|---|
| Collaboration | Cross-functional team spans engineering, AI research, safety, and operations |
| Scoping and prioritization | Complex feature set requires clear prioritization against safety and timeline |
| Operations | Understanding truck operations is essential to design a usable product |
| Market research | Must validate competitive landscape before committing to full development |
| Reliability engineering | AI system must meet high reliability standards in commercial vehicles |
| Quality systems management | Testing and validation frameworks are critical for safety-critical products |
| Artificial intelligence and data analytics | Core technology driving the product |
| Change management | Driver adoption requires structured change management and training |
| Product lifecycle management | Long development and certification cycle requires full lifecycle oversight |
| Strategic thinking | Aligning AI roadmap with company's long-term competitive positioning |
| Analytical skills | Data from testing and field use must inform product refinement |
| Communication skills | Must translate technical AI concepts to drivers, executives, and regulators |
| Project management skills | Managing dependencies across engineering, AI, safety, and regulatory workstreams |
| Business acumen | Cost-benefit analysis of development investment vs. competitive and revenue upside |
| Testing | Extensive field and simulation testing required before commercial deployment |
| UX design | Driver interface must be intuitive to minimize distraction and maximize adoption |
| Focus groups | Structured driver feedback sessions essential to validate usability |
| Relationship building | Long-term partnerships with AI researchers, regulators, and fleet operators |

## Key Takeaway

This lab made risk identification feel concrete in a way that abstract frameworks don't. Working through a safety-critical AI product where a wrong calculation doesn't just create a bad user experience but could cause a serious accident sharpened my thinking about how scope, prioritization, and compliance intersect. I also noticed how many of the skills I identified map directly to my existing operations background: cross-functional collaboration, change management, analytical skills, quality systems management, and project management. The skills I'm building toward are the product-specific ones: AI and data analytics literacy, UX thinking, and product lifecycle management at a technical level.
