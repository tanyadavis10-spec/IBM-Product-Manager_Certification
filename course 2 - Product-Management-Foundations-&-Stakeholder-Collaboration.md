## What This Course Covers

This course addresses one of the most important realities of product management - you spend more time managing people than managing products. This course builds the communication and collaboration skills that product managers rely on every day, including how to engage stakeholders with competing priorities, how to adapt your communication style to different audiences, and how to lead and influence without direct authority.

The course uses a case study methodology - presenting real-world scenarios where you evaluate what went right, what went wrong, and develop actionable recommendations. It integrates business frameworks including SWOT analysis, Porter's Five Forces, and Kotler's Five Levels of Product to build market and competitive thinking alongside stakeholder skills.

## My Biggest Insight From This Course

This course confirmed something I've seen firsthand across every program I've managed - communication breakdown is the root cause of most operational failures, not lack of skill or effort. The Communication Issues and Solutions lab made this concrete by showing that problems like role confusion, unprepared stakeholders, and unclear goals each have specific frameworks designed to prevent them. What struck me most is that in operations I solved these problems reactively - after they caused friction. Product management builds the communication infrastructure proactively, before problems surface. The RACI matrix, structured meeting formats, and clear product vision aren't administrative overhead - they are the systems that allow cross-functional teams to move fast without constant misalignment. This is the layer of product management that connects most directly to my operational background and where I have the most transferable depth.
